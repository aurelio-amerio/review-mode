# The Ultimate Chocolate Fudge Cake

This is a rich, moist, and deeply chocolatey cake that is perfect for any occasion. It's easy to make and guaranteed to be a crowd-pleaser.

## Prep & Cook Time
- **Prep time:** 20 mins
- **Cook time:** 30-35 mins
- **Serves:** 12 people

## Ingredients (for one cake)

### For the Cake
- 250g Plain Flour
- 350g Caster Sugar
- 85g High-quality Cocoa Powder
- 1 ½ tsp Baking Powder
- 1 ½ tsp Bicarbonate of Soda
- 2 Large Eggs
- 250ml Whole Milk
- 125ml Vegetable Oil
- 2 tsp Vanilla Extract
- 250ml Boiling Water

### For the Chocolate Ganache
- 200g Plain Chocolate (at least 70% cocoa solids)
- 200ml Double Cream

## Instructions

### 1. Preheat and Prepare
Preheat your oven to 180°C (160°C fan) / Gas Mark 4. Grease and line two 20cm (8 inch) round cake tins with baking parchment.

### 2. Mix Dry Ingredients
In a large mixing bowl, sift together the flour, sugar, cocoa powder, baking powder, and bicarbonate of soda. Whisk briefly to ensure everything is evenly combined.

### 3. Add Wet Ingredients
Add the eggs, milk, oil, and vanilla extract to the dry ingredients. Whisk with an electric hand mixer or a balloon whisk until smooth and well combined.

### 4. The Secret Step
Gradually pour in the boiling water while whisking at a low speed. The batter will become very thin and liquid—this is normal and is the secret to a moist cake!

### 5. Bake
Divide the batter equally between the two prepared tins. Bake for 30-35 minutes or until a skewer inserted into the center comes out clean.

### 6. Cool
Leave the cakes to cool in their tins for 10 minutes, then turn them out onto a wire rack to cool completely.

### 7. Make the Ganache
Break the chocolate into small pieces and place in a heatproof bowl. In a small saucepan, heat the double cream until it just begins to simmer. Pour the hot cream over the chocolate and let it sit for a few minutes before stirring until smooth and glossy. Let it cool and thicken slightly.

### 8. Assemble
Spread a layer of ganache over the top of one cake, place the second cake on top, and use the remaining ganache to cover the top and sides.

---

## Pro Tips
- **Quality Matters:** Use the best cocoa powder you can find; it makes a huge difference in the depth of chocolate flavor.
- **Don't Overbake:** Check the cake at 30 minutes. An overbaked chocolate cake can become dry.
- **Room Temperature:** Ensure your eggs and milk are at room temperature for the best emulsion.

## Variations
- **Coffee Infusion:** Add 1 tsp of instant espresso powder to the boiling water to intensify the chocolate flavor.
- **Fruit Twist:** Serve with fresh raspberries or a raspberry coulis to cut through the richness.
